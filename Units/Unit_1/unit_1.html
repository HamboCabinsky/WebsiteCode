According to the Humane Society pigs are smarter than dogs and 3 year old children.

One might notice that Piggy's intelligence is not appreciated by the other boys. It takes him a lot of effort to have his concerns considered and they're valid concerns given the gravity of the situation.

Pigs are intelligent enough to recognize their mortality. When pigs are slaughtered in the slaughterhouse, they are aware they are about to be killed and show fear.

In Chapter 1, Piggy seems a lot more worried than the other boys about the situation they are facing. Is this because Piggy is more intelligent and thus more aware of his mortality?

In the original Texas Chainsaw Massacre, the movie follows a family of murderers who have become desensitized to bloodshed as a result of their working at a slaughterhouse.

The 2022 Texas Chainsaw Massacre remake features a hero of the story who valiantly slaughters a pig on screen to introduce her to the story.

Is this a respectful thing to do considering the message of the original film?
